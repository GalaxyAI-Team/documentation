Galaxy.ai uses a credits-based system to allocate AI supercomputing resources fairly among users. Credits are not a fixed number per task — they reflect the actual computational load of each AI request, so more complex or resource-intensive generations consume more credits than lighter ones.

## How credits are deducted

When you use any AI feature — image generation, headshot creation, advanced GPT tasks, or video — credits are deducted based on the processing power and time required. Think of your credit balance like a bank account: the cost of each generation is drawn down automatically, and your remaining balance carries forward indefinitely.

You cannot predict upfront exactly how many credits a task will use. The actual cost depends on:

- The model you select
- Prompt length and complexity
- Output length or resolution
- Quality settings you choose

After each generation, a toast notification appears showing the number of credits consumed.

## Rollovers and renewals

Your monthly subscription credits roll over and never expire — unused credits accumulate in your account and are always available. On the same calendar date you originally subscribed each month, a fresh batch of credits is added to your balance.

If you purchase an add-on credit package, those credits also never expire and remain in your account until used.

<Note>
  Your renewal date stays consistent every month. For example, if you subscribed on March 15, your credits renew on April 15, May 15, and so on. Check your next renewal date in the billing portal or your account dashboard.
</Note>

## Lifetime plan credits

The Lifetime plan gives you **15 million credits per month for life** — this is not unlimited. Credits renew automatically every month. If you need more in a given month, you can purchase additional credits at any time.

With a Lifetime plan you also get access to all current and future AI models as Galaxy.ai adds them.

## Failed generations

You are never charged for a generation that fails to produce any output. Only successful generations count against your credits.

If a generation failed completely (no output was produced), credits are automatically refunded within 24–48 business hours. Verify refunded credits at [galaxy.ai/usage?show=credited](https://galaxy.ai/usage?show=credited).

<Warning>
  If a generation completed but the output was not what you expected, credits are not refundable — the computation was performed successfully. Only zero-output failures qualify for an automatic refund.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do unused credits roll over?">
    Yes. Both your monthly subscription credits and any add-on credits you purchase roll over indefinitely. They accumulate in your account and never expire.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="When do credits renew?">
    Credits are added every month on the same date you originally subscribed. Your billing cycle date stays consistent each month based on your original subscription date. If your credits have not renewed as expected, log out and back in to refresh your account data.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I get charged for failed generations?">
    No. If a generation fails to produce any output, you are not charged. Only successful outputs count against your credits. If a failure slips through and credits are deducted, you will receive an automatic refund within 24–48 business hours.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if a tool used millions of credits but produced no output?">
    Credits are automatically refunded within 24–48 business hours for complete failures. You can verify the refund at [galaxy.ai/usage?show=credited](https://galaxy.ai/usage?show=credited). If the generation completed (even partially), credits are not refundable per Galaxy.ai's policy.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
